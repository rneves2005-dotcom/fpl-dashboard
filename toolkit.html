01 · Decision Process
The filter you skip at your peril

The single biggest process lesson of the season: never do a 1-for-1 club swap without running the full filter. The Tavernier-vs-Scott miss happened because the short-circuit "replace Scott with another BOU asset" felt complete. It wasn't.

The 5-Step Filter
Run every transfer
  1. Fixture horizon (next 3-5 GWs). FDR weighted, check both DGW and blank risk. Bench cover included.
  2. Role security. On pens? On corners? On FKs? Starting 11 nailed? Check rotation risk for big clubs.
  3. Underlying numbers. xGI/90, xG, xA, threat, xDC — position-appropriate (see §02).
  4. Ownership / EO delta. What's the template carry? What's the opportunity cost if you fade?
  5. Price pressure. Rising/falling? Does delay cost £?
Anti-pattern to avoid: "I'm selling X from Club A, I'll buy Y from Club A." Club swaps only pass the filter when Y genuinely wins steps 1–4 on its own merits. Tavernier (FDR fine, set-pieces P2, xGI/90 0.47) was objectively superior to Scott (xGI/90 0.22). Process would have caught this. Shortcut didn't.
02 · Position-Specific Metrics
What matters for each role
Goalkeepers
GK · 1

Primary signals

  • xGC/90 (expected goals conceded) — ceiling of clean sheets
  • Saves/90 — busy keepers bank points even on defeats (3 saves = +1 pt)
  • Save pts rate — Saves/90 ÷ 3 (historical: Petrović, Dúbravka, Raya)
  • xCS% — derived from xGC via Poisson (FFH OPTA has this direct)

Tiebreakers

  • Pen save history (Raya, Pickford outliers)
  • Bonus magnet? (CBI + saves → BPS)
Defenders
DEF · 2

Primary signals

  • xCS% — fixture-adjusted, from FFH
  • xDC/90 — expected defensive contributions (threshold 10 for +2 pts)
  • xGI/90 — attacking threat for FB/WB
  • Set-piece role — corners P1/P2, FKs, direct threats

Archetypes

  • Nailed CB in top-6 defence: xCS + xDC (Gvardiol, Saliba)
  • Attacking FB: xGI dominant (Gabriel M, Lewis, Udogie)
  • Budget CB with xDC ceiling: Senesi, Tarkowski, Van Hecke
Midfielders
MID · 3

Primary signals

  • xGI/90 — single most predictive mid metric
  • Shots in Box/90 (OPTA) — separates decoys from real threats
  • Big Chances/90 — golden chance volume
  • Set-piece role — pen taker > direct FK > corners

xGI/90 tiers

  • Elite 0.55+ (Salah, Palmer in peak form)
  • Strong 0.40–0.55 (Saka, Son, Mbeumo)
  • Decent 0.30–0.40 (most premium mids)
  • Fade <0.25 (even if hot form, regression coming)
Forwards
FWD · 4

Primary signals

  • xG/90 — not xGI (forwards create less, score more)
  • Shots in Box/90 — crucial separator
  • Big Chances/90 — Haaland outlier live here
  • Pen role — P1 taker adds ~0.15 xG/90

xG/90 tiers

  • Elite 0.70+ (Haaland, Isak peak)
  • Strong 0.50–0.70 (Watkins, JP, Gyökeres)
  • Decent 0.35–0.50
  • Fade <0.30 (regress to mean or bench warmer)
03 · The xDC Framework
New scoring rule · huge edge if used

Defensive Contribution is new this season. Players earn +2 pts for hitting a position-specific threshold per 90. Most managers underuse it. Your live dashboard shows DC counts inline on both the pitch view and Match View.

Position Threshold What counts Best-in-class xDC/90
GK 12 CBI + recoveries rare — saves are already GK's pathway
DEF 10 CBI + tackles Tarkowski ~11, Senesi ~10, Van Hecke ~9
MID 12 CBI + tackles + recoveries Lewis-Skelly, Caicedo, Rodri ~13
FWD 12 CBI + tackles + recoveries very rare — only pressers like Jackson, Evanilson
Why this matters: A defender at xDC/90 ≥ 10 gets an almost-automatic +2 per week on top of CS + attacking. That's a 6-pt floor on weeks without CS, which is huge for "floor" consistency. Dollimore's modelling suggests xDC adds ~8-12 pts per DEF per half-season vs non-xDC peers.

08 · Retrospectives
Mistakes worth remembering
GW31 · Tavernier over Scott
Process fail

What happened: Sold Scott (BOU), bought another BOU mid (Scott → Scott replacement mindset). Ignored Tavernier who had xGI/90 of 0.47 (2× Scott's 0.22), was on BOU's P2 corners and P2 FKs, and cost similar.

Lesson: Club-swap heuristic isn't a transfer method. It's a shortcut that bypasses step 3 (underlying numbers) of the 5-step filter.

Cost estimate: ~8-12 pts over 4 weeks. Reversible by GW35 if Tavernier still available.

GW33 · FH against BBs
Context fail

What happened: Played FH in GW33. Miguel, Dennys, Anthoni all BB'd same GW. Their 15-man teams vs. our 11 meant baseline differential of ~30-40 pts against us before any captaincy variance.

Lesson: Chip selection must consider rivals' simultaneous chip use. If 3+ top-10 managers are BBing, FH ≠ fair comparison — they have a 4-player extra pool. Should've either matched with BB or waited a week.

Estève DC hit · GW33
xDC validation

Estève (BUR DEF) earned 4 FPL pts despite conceding 2 goals — because he hit the DC threshold (9 CBI + 1 tackle = 10). Without DC he'd have 1 pt. The Dollimore framework's xDC emphasis is paying out live.

Takeaway: Continue screening defenders on xDC/90 >= 8 (high-hit probability). Even in losses, 4-6 pt floors are possible.

GW37 · Wilson early-hook trap
Minutes-data fail

What happened: Started Wilson (FUL MID) in GW37 based on FFH 75' xMins. He came on as a sub at minute 79' and scored 1 pt. Meanwhile bench Virgil (14) and bench Watkins (15) sat unused — 27 pts on the bench. Lineup scored 46 pts vs an alternative ceiling of ~70.

Lesson: FFH xMins assumes baseline starter status. Wilson's L6 minutes log [79, 90, 90, 63, 61, 11] showed the early-hook trajectory clearly — declining mins, then a sub-cameo. Minutes data must override FFH xMins for any role decision.

Cost estimate: ~20+ pts in GW37 alone vs starting Watkins/Virgil. Triggered the "Minutes Audit" protocol addition in v3.

GW37 · LIV broken-defense signal missed
Multi-signal override fail

What happened: AVL vs LIV — Watkins benched despite LIV away GA 1.61 (worst-of-modern-era LIV defense), Salah ruled out as starter, AVL home + top-5 desperate. Four convergent positive signals for Watkins, all ignored because of UEL-final rotation fear. AVL 4-2 LIV, Watkins 2G + 1A = 15 pts on bench.

Lesson: When 3+ qualitative signals point the same direction (broken-defense + missing key opp player + max motivation + favourable home/away split), this is the override trigger condition. Trust the signal stack over the single rotation-risk concern.

Added as Rule #10 (Broken-Defense Override) and Rule #11 (In-Form Team Neutralizer) in v3.

Stale manager priors · multiple weeks
Prior-knowledge fail

What happened: Cited "Mbeumo at Brentford" (he's at MUN), "Andreas at Fulham" (left ~1yr ago), and "Amorim never subs B.Fern" (Carrick took over at MUN). Three separate stale-prior errors in two weeks of analysis.

Lesson: Never cite a manager's name or player-team pairing from prior knowledge. The L6 minutes log empirically captures whoever the current manager is and their rotation pattern. Data > priors. Always.

Encoded as a hard rule in v3: "Never cite manager names from prior knowledge — use minutes data."

GW38 · Cherki "hedge" caught pre-execution
User-flagged save

What almost happened: Proposed starting Cherki (MCI MID) in GW38 lineup as "correlation hedge" against rival Simao's Haaland captain. The reasoning relied on Cherki capturing MCI's offensive upside.

What the data actually said: Cherki's L4 fixtures = [59' start hooked, 11' sub, 34' sub] — clear bench-dropped pattern. Pep had phased him out with MCI's title slipping. A "hedge" that doesn't play isn't a hedge.

Lesson: Strategic narratives must always be cross-checked against minutes data. If a player's last 3 fixtures show bench/sub pattern, they cannot be in starting XI regardless of narrative justification. This is the master rule that prevents 80% of lineup mistakes.

GW38 · AVL UEL Final proximity buried
Context-weighting fail

What happened: AVL played UEL Final on Wed May 20 — only 4 days before GW38 deadline. Mentioned this in passing in early analysis but didn't make it the dominant variable for Watkins decision until user explicitly asked.

Lesson: Cup/Euro finals within ±5 days of a GW deadline are the single most predictive availability/motivation signal for the affected club's players. They must trigger explicit "DEFAULT BENCH until press-conf clarity" flagging at the top of the squad analysis — never as a footnote.

Encoded as Rule #8b (Cup-Final Proximity Check) in v3 protocol.

09 · Elite-Consistent Framework
What Hall of Fame managers actually do

Derived from analysing the FPL Scout Hall of Fame elite — Fábio Borges, Tom Stephenson, Alistair Hughes, Tom Dollimore, Josh Bull, Jonathan Ballantyne, Mark Sutherns, and others with 80+ top-10k finishes between them. What emerges is a layered framework: a diagnosis of style, a core of universal rules, then style-specific rules, and a list of anti-patterns everyone avoids.

Layer 1 · Temperament Diagnosis

Pick your style once, early in the season, and stick with it. Rotating philosophies mid-season is the single biggest predictor of mediocre finishes.

Dullard Style
Fábio · Stephenson · Sutherns

Profile

  • Time budget: 30-60 min/week
  • Goal: top 5-10k every season, rarely top 1k
  • Risk profile: variance-avoiding
  • Style: template-heavy, fixture-led, eye-test-first

Typical finish

Floor: top 40k (Stephenson never worse in 12 yrs)

Ceiling: top 100 very rare

Pick this if…

  • You want consistent annual top-10k finishes
  • You hate making transfers
  • You can ignore EO and "the template" complaints
  • You've been burned chasing hauls before
Operator Style
Dollimore · Hughes · Ballantyne · Bull

Profile

  • Time budget: 2-4 hrs/week
  • Goal: hunt top 1k, accept top 50k risk
  • Risk profile: variance-accepting
  • Style: underlying-stats-first, differentials welcome, model-driven

Typical finish

Ceiling: top 100 (Bull won #1 in 19/20)

Floor: top 50k but can be worse

Pick this if…

  • You have time for weekly data dives
  • You can hold through 50k+ rank drops without tilt-transferring
  • You enjoy differentials and model-building
  • You're comfortable being wrong often in pursuit of being very right occasionally

You built a dashboard instead of buying more tools. Transfer proposals are pre-planned (Semenyo → Ndiaye was decided weeks out), not reactive.

Layer 2 · Universal Rules (non-negotiable)

Every elite HoF manager follows these. Disagreement on style, agreement on these five:

  1. Hits must be +EV over your fixture horizon (3-5 GWs). A -4 must produce >4 expected pts above the alternative. Most weeks: no hit.
  2. Captain your premium unless FDR 5. Haaland/Salah by default. Rotation is a feature of bad managers. Alistair Hughes' most successful season: only 11 different captains across 38 GWs.
  3. Maximise first XI, minimise bench. £4.5m GK2, £4.0m bench players. Never overpay for a non-starting CB "just in case."
  4. Chips planned pre-season, deployed to fixture windows. Deliberate deployment only. Never "I've got BB, what GW should I use it?" — always "GW36 is the DGW, I'm using BB then."
  5. Sell weakness before buying strength. Your worst-performing squad member is the transfer, not the hottest player in the league.

Layer 3 · Style-Specific Rules

Dullard Rules
If you're Fábio-style
  • Fixtures > form for transfers (form follows fixtures)
  • Eye-test prioritised over underlying stats
  • Template tolerance: own the obvious premium captains
  • Fewer transfers is better — aim for ~15 total transfers across the season
  • No EO analysis except for captaincy
  • Accept variance — a 30k rank drop in one week is normal
  • Don't research past 45 min/week
Operator Rules
If you're Dollimore-style
  • xGI/90, xG/90, xDC/90 drive transfer decisions
  • OPTA-grade stats for nuanced calls (Shots in Box, Big Chances)
  • Differentials: 10-20% ownership sweet spot
  • Model fixture-adjusted expected points over 5 GW horizons
  • Accept some template misses for differential gains
  • xDC threshold for DEFs (≥8/90) is non-negotiable
  • Set-piece role is a first-class variable (pens > FKs > corners)

Layer 4 · Anti-Patterns (universal avoid)

Things NO elite manager does

  • Chasing last week's haul. Right-tail distribution outlier, not signal.
  • EO obsession outside captaincy. Effective ownership is a mental load tax without ROI.
  • Reactive tilt-transfers after a bad GW. Emotional transfers are the #1 killer of good seasons.
  • Analysis paralysis. Fábio: "I try not to get distracted by information overload."
  • Pivoting philosophy mid-season. Stephenson: "My worst season was when I didn't stick to my style."
  • Trying to out-captain the pack. Captain variance only helps if you're already template; adds risk otherwise.
  • Hits for the sake of hits. -4 is a 4-pt handicap. Most -4s fail to recoup.
  • "Shiny new toys" (Bull's phrase). The hot breakout player from last week is almost never the right buy.

10 · Weekly Scan Protocol
Data-first decision support · run every Tuesday + pre-deadline

Born from the post-GW36 retrospective. Several decision-quality gaps emerged across the GW33–GW36 cycle: late discovery of trending players (Doku surfaced weeks after his form turned), confabulated motivation tables (claimed "team X is locked" without checking points/games-left), missed European/cup commitments (Arsenal CL final, Aston Villa UEL final, Crystal Palace Conf final, Chelsea–City FA Cup final), and over-reliance on season-aggregate form when home/away splits told a different story. This protocol enforces data-first analysis to prevent each failure mode.

The core rule: data weight = 1, priors = 0. Every claim about standings, motivation, European status, or fixture difficulty must come from a fresh data pull. If data is unavailable, ASK. Never confabulate.

Cadence

  • Tuesday morning (post-weekend GW): full scan — outcome retrospective + GW+1 prep + GW+2 forward look
  • Friday pre-deadline: refresh scan — confirm captain, lock transfers, final commitment
  • Mid-week (after midweek fixtures): quick check if cup/Euro matches changed rotation picture

What gaps this protocol closes

  • Trending players surfaced LATE: Section 5 (Ranked Transfer Suggestions) forces proactive surfacing of YOUR best moves each week, not reactive responding to questions
  • Confabulated context: mandatory data pulls mean league standings, European fixtures, motivation indices come from URLs, not memory
  • Season-aggregate form misleading: mandatory home/away split pulls — applied to BOTH teams in every fixture analysis
  • Captain oscillation: FFH predicted points are the primary input, with the 9-point framework as sanity check — no more vibes-based flips
  • Rough EV estimates: FFH `/predictions` page provides the authoritative xPTS data, scraped per scan — eliminates the need for hand-rolled fixture-quality math
  • Cross-position transfer noise: position-feasibility is now an explicit rule (MID→MID, DEF→DEF) — prevents suggesting Semenyo→Truffert-type swaps
  • Marginal-move noise: +2.0 net EV minimum threshold — below that is variance, not signal
  • Loose terminology: precise glossary baked into prompt (DGW vs BGW vs DNP vs low return)
